CMMC Level 2 represents the certification tier that the majority of defense industrial base (DIB) organizations will need to achieve to maintain their DoD contracts. It maps directly to the 110 security practices defined in NIST SP 800-171, spanning 14 control families.

## What Is CMMC Level 2?

Level 2 is designed to protect Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) — the sensitive but unclassified data that flows through defense supply chains. If your organization handles CUI, Level 2 certification is almost certainly in your future.

Unlike Level 1 (which only requires an annual self-assessment), Level 2 requires a triennial third-party assessment conducted by a C3PAO — a Certified Third Party Assessment Organization like Init Cyber.

## The 14 Control Families

CMMC Level 2 encompasses 110 practices organized across 14 domains:

1. **Access Control (AC)** — 22 practices
2. **Awareness and Training (AT)** — 3 practices
3. **Audit and Accountability (AU)** — 9 practices
4. **Configuration Management (CM)** — 9 practices
5. **Identification and Authentication (IA)** — 11 practices
6. **Incident Response (IR)** — 3 practices
7. **Maintenance (MA)** — 6 practices
8. **Media Protection (MP)** — 9 practices
9. **Personnel Security (PS)** — 2 practices
10. **Physical Protection (PE)** — 6 practices
11. **Risk Assessment (RA)** — 3 practices
12. **Security Assessment (CA)** — 4 practices
13. **System and Communications Protection (SC)** — 16 practices
14. **System and Information Integrity (SI)** — 7 practices

## How to Prepare

The best starting point is a thorough gap assessment. You need to understand where you stand against all 110 practices before you can build a realistic remediation plan.

Key steps include:

- **Define your CUI boundary** — Know exactly what systems touch CUI and scope your assessment accordingly
- **Conduct a gap assessment** — Compare your current controls against every NIST 800-171 requirement
- **Build a System Security Plan (SSP)** — Document your environment and how you meet each requirement
- **Create a Plan of Action & Milestones (POA&M)** — Track gaps and your plan to close them
- **Implement and document** — Close your gaps and build evidence of compliance
