If you hold a DoD contract that involves Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI), you almost certainly have a Supplier Performance Risk System (SPRS) score — whether you know it or not.

## What Is an SPRS Score?

SPRS is the DoD's system for tracking contractor performance risk. For cybersecurity purposes, your SPRS score reflects your self-assessed compliance with NIST SP 800-171, which is the foundational framework for CMMC Level 2.

Your score ranges from **-203 to +110**. The maximum score of 110 means you've fully implemented all 110 NIST 800-171 practices. Each unimplemented practice reduces your score based on its weighted severity.

## How Is It Calculated?

Each of the 110 NIST 800-171 practices is assigned a point value based on its criticality. Failing to implement a practice subtracts those points from your score.

For example:
- A missing multi-factor authentication control carries a higher weight than a missing low-criticality documentation requirement
- Organizations with no cybersecurity program can score as low as -203

## Why It Matters

**Contracting Officers look at your SPRS score.** A low score can trigger additional scrutiny, delayed contract awards, or disqualification from contracts entirely.

**DFARS 252.204-7012** requires contractors to have current NIST 800-171 assessments on file with SPRS. If you haven't submitted a score, you may be in violation of your existing contracts.

**Your score is self-reported today, but CMMC changes that.** Under CMMC, Level 2 organizations will need a validated C3PAO assessment — your self-reported score won't be sufficient for new contracts.

## How to Improve Your Score

1. **Start with a gap assessment** — Understand exactly which practices you haven't implemented
2. **Prioritize high-weight gaps** — Focus remediation on the practices that most impact your score
3. **Document what you have** — Many contractors have controls in place but haven't documented them, losing credit
4. **Build and submit your SSP** — Your System Security Plan supports your SPRS score submission
