# Before You Spend Six Figures on CMMC, Read This.

Most defense contractors overspend on CMMC compliance because they start in the wrong place. Here’s how to fix that… with two fixed-fee or hourly engagements designed to give you clarity before you commit.

If you’re a defense contractor handling Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI), CMMC Level 2 certification isn’t optional, it’s a contract requirement. And if you’re like most small and mid-sized defense industrial base (DIB) companies, you’ve probably heard the number: a full CMMC compliance program can run **$75,000 to $250,000 or more** before you ever sit down with a C3PAO assessor.

That number scares people into one of two bad decisions: they either ignore CMMC until a contract forces the issue, or they rush into a full engagement without understanding their actual gaps, their real scope, or what it’s actually going to cost to get certified.

Both paths are expensive. Both are avoidable.

## The Real Problem: Starting in the Wrong Place

After working with dozens of DIB contractors across the Southeast, we’ve seen the same two failure patterns over and over:

**▲ Failure Pattern 1: Scope Creep from Day One.** The organization treats their entire IT environment as in-scope — every workstation, every cloud service, every user — without ever asking whether CUI actually flows through all of it. The result: a remediation bill three times larger than it needed to be, because they never defined the boundary before they started fixing things.

**▲ Failure Pattern 2: Flying Blind into a C3PAO Assessment.** The organization hires a consultant, spends months remediating, documents everything — and then the assessor finds gaps that were never on the radar, or challenges the scope definition, or flags a system that wasn’t in the SSP. Assessment delayed. Conditional certification. More time, more cost.

The fix for both of these isn’t a bigger compliance budget. It’s **starting with the right information**.

## Common Questions

**Are these official CMMC assessments?** No. Only a C3PAO can conduct an official CMMC Level 2 assessment. These are advisory pre-engagement services that prepare you for that process and dramatically reduce your risk of surprise findings.

**Can these costs be treated as allowable under our DoD contracts?** Generally yes — CMMC readiness consulting typically qualifies as allowable professional services under FAR 31.205-33. Confirm with your contracts team for contract-specific guidance.

**We already have an MSP managing our IT. Do we still need this?** Yes — often more so. Managed environments frequently have the most ambiguous CUI boundaries, especially with shared tenants, co-managed Intune, or split DNS/AD configurations. We’ll work with your MSP to get accurate data flows documented.
